Wireframe Sites

"What problem is the web page solving? What visual challenges are there?"

Codecademy

This web page offers the user the opportunity to learn how to code for free. The problem that is solved is the main question: what does this website immediately offer the user? This website immediately gives a statement of purpose and a call to action in the form of a pink "sign-up" button. The visual challenge could also be its greatest strength. After staying on the home page for a while, the website can seem like a dull grey slate; however, the pink button is quick to draw attention because there is nothing else on the site to pay attention.

.

Apple

A company as reputable as Apple can afford to pay less attention to giving its customer a statement of purpose and more on a streamlined design that can direct the customer to more information on its famous products. Right away, it's most recent and popular product is available in the form of a graphic that provides a link to a video that offers more information. The bottom displays more graphics that provide avenues to view more of Apple's recent developments and innovations. For customers with a more specific interest in mind, the top menu bar succintly lays out links to company product pages.

.

Manga Stream

A website that posts scans of new chapter releases of various manga series should find a way to effectively display new chapters that have been posted. Manga Stream has found a way to do that in a way that draws the user's eye rather quickly but allows space for a blog. There's also a visual battle between space and purpose. Oftentimes, I find that the object that takes up the most space draws the most attention. Depending on the goal of the developer/designer, they might want to adjust the positioning of the table of manga chapter posts and blog entries.
